## What Changed

**humanly v2.0** now includes automatic humanization of all outputs using the **humanizer skill** from [@blader](https://github.com/blader/humanizer).

Every piece of content generated by humanly is now automatically processed through a 24-pattern AI detection and removal system, then enhanced with soul and personality.

---

## The Problem humanly v1 Had

Even with our "humanly style" guidelines (no hype, no FOMO, story-driven), AI-generated content still showed patterns:

- "Additionally..." everywhere
- "serves as" instead of "is"
- Rule of three (always groups of 3 items)
- Em dash overuse
- Generic conclusions ("The future looks bright")
- Lack of personality and soul

**Result:** Content was better than raw AI, but still sounded... algorithmic.

---

## The Solution: Integrated Humanizer

Now every output goes through a two-phase process:

### Phase 1: humanly Transformation
- Story-driven
- No hype, no FOMO
- Pure value and wisdom
- Soft, simple language

### Phase 2: Humanizer Post-Processing
- **Removes 24 AI patterns** (see list below)
- **Adds soul and personality**
- Varies sentence structure
- Injects opinions and feelings
- Acknowledges complexity
- Makes it sound like a real human wrote it

---

## 24 AI Patterns Removed

### Content Patterns
1. **Significance inflation** - "pivotal moment", "testament to", "crucial role"
2. **Notability name-dropping** - Listing sources without context
3. **Superficial -ing analyses** - "symbolizing", "reflecting", "showcasing"
4. **Promotional language** - "nestled", "vibrant", "boasts"
5. **Vague attributions** - "Experts believe", "Industry reports"
6. **Formulaic challenges** - "Despite challenges... continues to thrive"

### Language Patterns
7. **AI vocabulary** - "Additionally", "crucial", "landscape", "showcase"
8. **Copula avoidance** - "serves as" â†’ "is", "features" â†’ "has"
9. **Negative parallelisms** - "It's not just X, it's Y"
10. **Rule of three** - Always grouping in threes
11. **Synonym cycling** - "protagonist... main character... hero..."
12. **False ranges** - "from X to Y" when just listing

### Style Patterns
13. **Em dash overuse** - Too many â€” in sentences
14. **Boldface overuse** - **Everything** is **bold**
15. **Inline-header lists** - Repetitive structure
16. **Title Case Headings** - Wrong Capitalization

### Communication Patterns
19. **Chatbot artifacts** - "I hope this helps!"
20. **Cutoff disclaimers** - "While details are limited..."
21. **Sycophantic tone** - "Great question! You're absolutely right!"

### Filler and Hedging
22. **Filler phrases** - "In order to", "Due to the fact that"
23. **Excessive hedging** - "could potentially possibly"
24. **Generic conclusions** - Vague wrap-ups

## Soul and Personality Added

Beyond just removing bad patterns, humanizer adds human qualities:

### What It Adds:
- **Opinions** - "I genuinely don't know how to feel about this"
- **Varied rhythm** - Short punchy sentences. Then longer ones that flow.
- **Complexity** - Acknowledging mixed feelings and uncertainty
- **First person** - Using "I" when it fits the voice
- **Specific feelings** - Not "concerning" but "unsettling"
- **Natural mess** - Tangents, asides, half-formed thoughts

## Technical Details

### Architecture

1. **Input:** URL provided by user
2. **Fetch:** Content extracted from URL (web_fetch)
3. **Transform:** humanly style applied (story-driven, no hype)
4. **Humanize:** 24 patterns removed + soul added (humanizer)
5. **Output:** Final humanized content saved to file

### Integration Method

The humanizer is embedded directly into the humanly_v2 script as a second-phase prompt. When the OpenClaw agent processes a request:

1. Phase 1: Generate content in humanly style
2. Phase 2: Pass output through humanizer prompt
3. Result: Doubly-processed, ultra-human content
